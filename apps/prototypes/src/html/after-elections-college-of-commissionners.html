College of commissionners

The College of Commissioners, composed of the President of the European Commission and 26 other Commissioners, leads the European Commission. They propose new laws, manage the EUâ€™s daily operations, and ensure policies are implemented correctly.

The President is selected by the European Council and approved by the European Parliament, while the other Commissioners are nominated by EU member states and must also be approved by the European Parliament.

The Treaty establishing the European Community requires that the Members of the European Commission receive approval from the European Parliament before they are appointed. To do this, each Commissioner-designate goes through hearings with the relevant parliamentary committees.
