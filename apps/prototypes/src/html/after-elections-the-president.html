The President of the European Parliament

The President of the European Parliament leads and represents the European Parliament in all legal matters and at international events. The President manages parliamentary debates and activities, ensuring that discussions are orderly and rules are followed. The President also signs key documents, like the EU budget and laws passed by the Parliament. The role of ther President is crucial for making sure the Parliament runs smoothly and that the voices of the MEPs are heard.

The President is elected by Members of the European Parliament through a secret ballot. The term lasts two and a half years, and the President can be re-elected for another term.
